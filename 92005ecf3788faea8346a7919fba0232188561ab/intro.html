INTRODUCTION

    This app is different from most other apps in two respects:
    First, it offers multiple solutions, as opposed to, say, meditation apps that offer only meditation as a solution.
    Secondly, it explains why the solutions are likely to help, which may reduce skepticism that they work.

    For most negative emotions, the emotional reaction is more than needed. If you want to know why, click here.
    So the first step to control most negative emotions is to tell yourself that you are overreacting.

    The second step is to identify the cause of the emotion, which is usually easy (e.g. stress due to a deadline), take steps to address the cause (when possible), and then shoo away the emotion by reminding yourself that you have done all that is possible to address the cause.

    If the above does not work fully, the next step is to distract yourself. To understand why this should work, click on the link mentioned two paragraphs above, which is repeated here.

    The three steps above are usually common in dealing with all negative emotions, so you will see them repeated. But for most negative emotions, some extra steps have to be taken that are specific to that emotion, which will be mentioned when you click on that particular emotion. You may think of this app as your friend in need.

    The suggestions of this app may not work immediately to the extent that you would like, and it may require you to use the ideas from this app several times till they sink in and are effective. As mentioned in te links given above, negative emotions should not be ignored, but have to be dealt with. With that in mind, the goal of this app is not to get rid of negative emotions, but to bring you to a better place, where you are able to handle negative emotions without completely ignoring them. Once you reach that point, you may not need the app on a regular basis. At the same time, to borrow a phrase from Stephen Covey, it is important to keep "sharpening the saw". So keep using the app as needed so that you stay on the right path.
